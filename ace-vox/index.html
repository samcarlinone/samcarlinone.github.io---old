Postmortem

Technically this project isn’t finished. Originally I had a grand vision of a game that took many of the basic ideas from Minecraft but would greatly improve on the Redstone system to allow much greater automation. Also it runs in your browser which is actually a feature Minecraft did have at one point. Still, I haven’t worked on it in a while and I’m not sure if I will any time soon.

I like the project; however, it had quite a few fundamental problems. I found the way I set up pseudo-multithreading with WebWorkers worked, but was kind of messy. In addition, collision detection was proving harder than anticipated. The mesh generation code was huge and very painful to work with. Furthermore, the idea of multiplayer had always been in the back of my mind. But I was already laying the foundation and hadn’t actually thought about how I would handle any of the unique challenges that come with multiplayer.

Because of this I decided to take a break and come back at some point in the future. I am still learning a lot about graphics tech. And the size of the project meant that debugging anything was harder than it should be. I still like the concept, and I think modern browsers are up to the task. But at least right now I lack the experience and time to do it justice.
